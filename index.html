SRC kinase drives multidrug resistance induced by KRAS-G12C inhibition
High
A genome-wide CRISPR screen showed that adagrasib (MRTX849) induces multidrug resistance in NSCLC by upregulating the ABCC1 transporter via SRC-mediated JUN activation. The SRC inhibitor dasatinib was identified as a combination partner that potentiates MRTX849 efficacy and suppresses this resistance pathway in patient-derived organoids.
Why this matters to you Identifies a specific, druggable resistance mechanism — if your programme targets post-G12Ci failure patients, this is directly relevant to combination strategy.
📄 Science Advances 📅 Dec 2024 👥 Okumura et al.
RAS-ON inhibition overcomes clinical resistance to KRAS G12C-OFF covalent blockade
High
Using patient-derived models from a sotorasib-resistant NSCLC case, the authors show that increased KRAS-GTP loading drives adaptive resistance to OFF-state inhibitors. RMC-6291, a clinical-stage KRAS G12C(ON) inhibitor, alone or combined with OFF-state drugs, restored tumour suppression in these resistant models.
Why this matters to you Proof-of-concept for RAS(ON) inhibitors as a next-generation approach — directly relevant if your programme targets the GTP-bound state or combination sequencing.
📄 Nature Communications 📅 Aug 2024 👥 Nokin, Mira et al.
Tumor suppressor gene co-mutations orchestrate resistance to KRAS G12C inhibitors in NSCLC
Watch
Review and analysis showing that co-occurring mutations in STK11/LKB1, KEAP1, TP53, and CDKN2A/B are the dominant drivers of primary and acquired resistance, working through sustained proliferation, metabolic reprogramming, and immune microenvironment remodelling. No single bypass pathway accounts for resistance — it is heterogeneous.

US12291539B2 · Granted Mar 2025
Dual KRAS G12C (ON) and (OFF) inhibitor compounds for treatment of resistant cancers
Assignee: Frontier Medicines Corporation
Granted
Claims covering small molecule compounds designed to bind and inhibit both GDP-bound and GTP-bound forms of KRAS G12C — directly targeting the limitation of current approved OFF-state inhibitors. Broad claims across NSCLC, CRC, and PDAC.
⚠️ Broad dual-state inhibition claims — if your approach targets KRAS in either conformational state, this is worth a freedom-to-operate read.
PCT/US2022/079324 · National stage filed 2024
KRAS G12C inhibitor compounds with activity against resistance mutations
Assignee: Frontier Medicines Corporation
Application
Method and composition claims for next-generation G12C inhibitors designed to retain activity against secondary resistance mutations (R68S, Y96C) that arise under sotorasib and adagrasib treatment. Continuation of an active filing programme.
🧭 Where the field is heading
KRAS G12C research is bifurcating. One branch is pursuing vertical pathway co-inhibition (G12C + SHP2, MEK, or SOS1) to suppress adaptive reactivation — where most current IP is clustering. The other — faster-growing in academic output and now with clinical proof-of-concept — is focused on non-covalent RAS(ON) inhibitors designed to work after covalent inhibitor failure. Frontier Medicines' granted dual ON/OFF patent and Revolution Medicines' clinical data are converging signals that the next-gen direct approach is being validated in parallel by multiple independent programmes.

Field signal this week

A multi-centre ctDNA study from Dana-Farber and MD Anderson identified KRAS amplification as the dominant acquired resistance mechanism after sotorasib treatment — detected in 34% of progressive patients. Concurrent submissions to two major journals suggest this finding is about to shift front-line combination trial design.

STK11/KEAP1 co-mutations predict intrinsic resistance to KRAS G12C inhibitors independent of PD-L1 status in NSCLC
High
MD Anderson/Vanderbilt retrospective of 312 KRAS G12C NSCLC patients found STK11 loss correlated with primary resistance to both sotorasib and adagrasib (ORR 4% vs 42% in wild-type). KEAP1 co-mutation further worsened outcomes. Authors propose a biomarker triage algorithm for first-line treatment selection.
Why this matters to you Directly challenges the assumption that G12C status alone is sufficient for patient selection — relevant if your programme includes biomarker-stratified claims or companion diagnostic positioning.
📄 Nature Medicine 📅 Apr 17, 2025 👥 Skoulidis et al.
Acquired KRAS G12C/G12D co-mutation as a novel on-target resistance mechanism to covalent G12C inhibitors identified by longitudinal ctDNA profiling
High
MSK/Dana-Farber ctDNA analysis of 87 patients progressing on sotorasib or adagrasib identified a novel G12D secondary mutation in 11% of cases — potentially non-crossresistant to non-covalent inhibitors. First prospective evidence this variant is selected for under treatment pressure.
Why this matters to you Opens a resistance escape route that non-covalent or pan-KRAS approaches could address — watch for IP activity around G12D in this context.
📄 Cancer Discovery 📅 Apr 14, 2025 👥 Awad, Ou et al.
SOS1 inhibition synergises with KRAS G12C blockade to suppress adaptive RAS reactivation in NSCLC models
Watch
Boehringer Ingelheim-affiliated group demonstrated that BI-3406 (SOS1 inhibitor) substantially blunts adaptive feedback that limits G12C inhibitor durability in vitro and in PDX models. Combination index was favourable across six NSCLC cell lines tested. No clinical data yet.

US2025/0191203 A1 · Filed Apr 8, 2025
Combination therapy comprising KRAS G12C inhibitor and SHP2 inhibitor for treatment of KRAS-mutant cancers resistant to prior KRAS inhibition
Assignee: Amgen Inc.
Application
Broad claims covering co-administration of sotorasib with RMC-4630 or TNO155 class SHP2 inhibitors. Method claims extend to re-treatment after acquired resistance. Strong overlap with combination strategies in active IND applications.
⚠️ Claim language around "re-treatment after acquired KRAS inhibitor resistance" is unusually broad — could read on any salvage combination approach in this space. Worth a legal review.
EP25181047 · Filed Apr 15, 2025
Tri-complex inhibitors targeting RAS(ON) G12C for treatment of cancers with acquired resistance to covalent KRAS G12C inhibitors
Assignee: Revolution Medicines Inc.
Application
European application covering RAS(ON) inhibitor scaffolds (non-covalent, active-state binders) with specific claims for use in patients who have progressed on sotorasib or adagrasib. Builds on published RMC-6236 Phase I data.
🧭 Where the field is heading
KRAS G12C research is bifurcating. One branch is pursuing vertical pathway co-inhibition (G12C + SHP2, MEK, or SOS1) to suppress adaptive reactivation — where most current IP is clustering. The other — faster-growing in academic output — is focused on non-covalent RAS(ON) inhibitors designed to work after covalent inhibitor failure. That gap may represent both a freedom-to-operate opportunity and a signal that the next-gen direct approach is harder to validate clinically.
